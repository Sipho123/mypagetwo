    RFID Hospitality

    RFID tag helps hotels in understanding their customers. Few hotels offer RFID enabled cards to their frequent travelers. These RFID cards work as a room key. Some hotel brands provide RFID enabled wristbands, through which they keep track of their guest activity..

    RFID Spaza Shop

    Retailers are adopting RFID technology in large numbers to ensure instant product availability by raising their inventory accuracy to over 98%. Retailers adopt RFID for both inventory and supply chain management visibility, which is based on tagging all their products with RFID labels. Tagging products automatically detect suspicious events such as items leaving the store in bulk or single items more likely to be stolen. Track every unique item at critical areas in your stores, such as at the point-of-sale or your store entrance and exit. After your RFID-based system identifies one or more items that are leaving the store without being paid for, it RINGS Alarm Notification to make the retailder aware. RFID ordering system helps the retailer to order arcording to movent of stock to prevent non-moving items sitting on chelves for long. RFID adoption also creates an opportunity to optimize other store processes such as data collection and faster checkouts.

    RFID in Assets Tracking

    RFID asset management systems allow you to keep track of individual items through the use of RFID tags attached to them. The tags are read by fixed scanners installed on doorways and other key points or by staff using mobile phones or tablets..

    in Automation

    Automobile manufacturers use RFID to track components during assembly and with completed vehicles post-assembly. Auto dealers utilize the technology to track their inventory of vehicles for sale. Dealerships also use RFID to make vehicle service more efficient, and to personally greet customers when they arrive.

    RFID in Warehousing

    RFID tags can tell you when items come in to or leave the warehouse: RFID readers can be placed at entrances and exits to your facility, allowing real-time tracking of inventory as it moves in and out of your warehouse. You can know immediately when a shipment has entered the warehouse, and track when items leave.
